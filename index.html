Case Study 001

Water U Doing?

Feel the thrill of competition while conquering your hydration goals.

Staying hydrated is an extremely important aspect to maintain a person's health. Unfortunately, many people forget to or are unmotivated to drink water throughout the day.


With this in mind, my team and I took the inititiative and designed the "Water U Doing?" mobile app to address this. My involvements in this project include: conducting semi-structured interviews, designing low fidelity prototypes, user testing, transforming low fidelity prototypes to high fidelity prototypes, and add UI interactions to the high fidelity prototype.

Overview

THE PROBELM

Did you know that 75% of people are chronically dehydrated?


THE SOLUTION

In order to address this, my team and I designed a mobile app that help users drink more water through connecting them and creating a community of health conscious individuals so that they can reach their hydration goals together.

The Product

Home page introduces challenges while displaying the user's water intake amount for the day and the user's recommended water intake amount.

Add water page allows users to quickly input their water intake with recommended amount or type in their own.

Challenges show both user and their challenger's water intake of the day. It also keeps track of the user's wins and losses.

User Research

UNDERSTANDING PEOPLE

First, my team and I crafted and sent out a survey on social media to ask about their experience with staying hydrated and drinking water. We recieved about 65 responses and found that many people have experienced symptoms of dehydration and believed that they were not properly hydrating throughout the day. In order to learn more, my team and I then created an interview plan with questions that would help us dive deeper into this trend and understand why it was occurring.

We found 8 people to interview about their experience with hydration with the requirements that they were a young adult (between 18-30 years old) who was concerned about their health. Semi-structured interviews revealed that many people are simply not motivated to drink water.


Interview Plan / Survey Results
KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS
  1. People are not motivated to drink water, they are not necessarily forgetting to drink water.
  2. People understand that staying hydrated is important for their health and are willing to drink water.
  3. People have trouble remembering how much water they have drank and do not know how much water they need to drink in order to stay hydrated.
  4. 75% of people reported feeling symtpoms of dehydration to the point that it negative affects them.
USER PERSONA: MEET SHELBY JONES

From our research we created our user persona, Shelby Jones. Shelby prioritizes her health but struggles to consistently stay hydrated due to lack of motivation.
